Lorenz Attractor & Butterfly Effect

◎ Interact

Two identical campaigns, a microsecond apart, produce wildly different outcomes. Mathematical proof that A/B testing isn't optional — it's mandatory for survival in non-linear markets.

dx/dt = σ(y−x),   dy/dt = x(ρ−z)−y,   dz/dt = xy−βz


σ = brand presence, ρ = market demand, β = churn rate. These three constants determine whether your pipeline enters a stable attractor or chaotic divergence. The two-lobe attractor maps to two primary conversion modes: organic and paid — forever coupled, never identical.
